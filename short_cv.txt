Martin Farkas received his MS degree with honors in software engineering from the Budapest University of Technology and Economics and he is currently a PhD student at the Fault Tolerant Systems Research Group. His research topic is the model driven design of decentralized trust solutions. This covers the development of novel protocols and collaboration patterns utilizing modern cryptographic tools such as Zero-Knowledge Proofs, and emerging Self-Sovereign Identity standards. He participated in several research and development projects in the field of enterprise decentralized ledged technologies. He also presented his work at several conferences, including MASCOTS'24 and BPM'25.
